# Customer Journey Map for Debug-Flow

## Phase 1: Aware
- **Trigger Event**: User encounters a debugging issue in Python while working on a scientific project.
- **Friction Points**: Lack of awareness about collaborative debugging tools; overwhelming number of resources and tools available.
- **User Emotions**: Frustration and confusion due to debugging challenges; curiosity about potential solutions.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Targeted marketing campaigns showcasing success stories of users who resolved similar issues with Debug-Flow; educational content (blogs, webinars) explaining the benefits of collaborative debugging.
- **Metric**: Number of website visits and engagement with marketing content.

## Phase 2: Consider
- **Trigger Event**: User researches solutions and finds Debug-Flow through search engines or recommendations.
- **Friction Points**: Uncertainty about the effectiveness of the platform; comparison with existing tools; concerns about ease of use for non-technical users.
- **User Emotions**: Hopeful yet skeptical; weighing options and looking for validation.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Provide free trials or demos; user testimonials and case studies; clear comparison charts highlighting unique features of Debug-Flow.
- **Metric**: Number of demo sign-ups and inquiries.

## Phase 3: Try
- **Trigger Event**: User signs up for a free trial or demo of Debug-Flow.
- **Friction Points**: Initial setup complexity; learning curve associated with new tools; potential technical issues during onboarding.
- **User Emotions**: Excitement mixed with anxiety; eagerness to solve debugging issues but apprehensive about usability.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Intuitive onboarding process with guided tutorials; responsive customer support; community forums for peer assistance.
- **Metric**: Trial conversion rate and user engagement during the trial period.

## Phase 4: Adopt
- **Trigger Event**: User successfully resolves debugging issues using Debug-Flow and recognizes its value.
- **Friction Points**: Integration with existing workflows; potential resistance to change from team members.
- **User Emotions**: Satisfaction and relief from solving problems; increased confidence in using the tool.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Personalized onboarding sessions for teams; showcasing advanced features that enhance collaboration; regular check-ins for feedback and support.
- **Metric**: User retention rate and frequency of tool usage.

## Phase 5: Expand
- **Trigger Event**: User sees additional features or capabilities that could benefit their team or projects.
- **Friction Points**: Budget constraints; hesitation to invest in additional features without proven ROI.
- **User Emotions**: Enthusiasm for potential growth; concern about costs and resource allocation.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Offer tiered pricing plans; highlight success metrics from existing users; provide insights on how expanded features can lead to time savings and increased productivity.
- **Metric**: Upsell conversion rate and customer lifetime value.
